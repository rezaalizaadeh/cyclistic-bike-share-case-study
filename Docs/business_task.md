# Business Task – Cyclistic Case Study  

Cyclistic wants to increase the number of annual members because they are more profitable than casual riders.  
To support this goal, the marketing team must understand how annual members and casual riders use the bike-share system differently.

---

## Business Objective  

Analyze 12 months of historical Cyclistic (Divvy) trip data to:  

1. Identify key differences in usage patterns between **casual riders** and **annual members**.  
2. Translate these differences into **data-driven insights** for the marketing strategy.  
3. Propose **three concrete recommendations** for converting more casual riders into annual members.

---

## Primary Question  

**How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?**

---

## Secondary Questions  

1. How do ride **duration** and **trip frequency** differ between casual riders and members?  
2. How does usage differ by **day of week** and **time of day** for each rider type?  
3. Are there specific **months or seasons** when casual riders are more active than members?  
4. Are certain **stations** or **areas** used more by casual riders compared to members?  
5. Which patterns are most relevant for designing **targeted membership campaigns**?

---

## Scope & Assumptions  

- Analysis is based solely on **trip-level data**; no personally identifiable information (PII) is available.  
- Only the **previous 12 months** of data are used (Nov 2024 – Oct 2025).  
- Results will be used to support **marketing decisions**, not operational or pricing changes.
